Beef Carpaccio

Description

Carpaccio is a typical dish of Italian cuisine, made with raw beef or fish, cut into very thin slices, seasoned with olive oil, lemon juice, and sprinkled with parmesan or pecorino shavings.

Ingredients

  • 25 oz tender loin
  • olive oil
  • juice of 1 lemon
  • Parmigiano Reggiano
  • fresh arugula
  • salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Combine olive oil, lemon juice, fleur de sel, and ground pepper in a bowl and whisk until emulsified. Set aside.
  2. Place the piece of beef in the freezer without freezing it completely.
  3. Using a ham slicer or, failing that, a knife with a long, thin, sharp blade, slice the meat into very thin slices.
  4. If the slices are still too thick, place them between two pieces of cling film, and flatten with a rolling pin, being careful not to crush the meat too much.
  5. Divide the beef slices among 4 plates, and drizzle with the dressing.
  6. Sprinkle with parmesan.
  7. Serve immediately decorated with fresh arugula leaves.
